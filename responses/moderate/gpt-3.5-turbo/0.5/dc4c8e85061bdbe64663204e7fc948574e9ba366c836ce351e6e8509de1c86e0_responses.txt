2. Control systems: Nuclear reactors are equipped with control rods that can absorb neutrons and regulate the rate of fission reactions. This helps to prevent the reactor from overheating and ensures a stable and controlled release of energy.

3. Safety measures: Nuclear power plants are designed with multiple safety systems to prevent accidents and mitigate their consequences. These include measures to prevent meltdowns, contain radioactive materials, and protect against external threats such as natural disasters or terrorist attacks.
