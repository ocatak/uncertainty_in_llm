One of the most significant effects of climate change on ecosystems is the loss of biodiversity. Another effect of climate change on ecosystems is the disruption of natural habitats. Climate change also has impacts on ecosystem services, such as pollination, water purification, and carbon sequestration. Changes in temperature and precipitation patterns can disrupt these services, leading to declines in agricultural productivity, water quality, and the ability of ecosystems to store carbon and mitigate climate change.

Overall, the causes and effects of climate change on global ecosystems are interconnected and complex. It is essential to address the root causes of climate change by reducing greenhouse gas emissions and transitioning to sustainable practices to protect and preserve ecosystems for future generations.
