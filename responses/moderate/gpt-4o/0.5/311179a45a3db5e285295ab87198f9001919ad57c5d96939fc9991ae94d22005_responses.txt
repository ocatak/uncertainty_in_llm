5. **Economic Policies and Reforms**: Many countries have adopted economic policies that favor market liberalization, privatization, and deregulation. These policies are designed to attract foreign investment and integrate national economies into the global market.

6. **International Institutions**: Organizations such as the International Monetary Fund (IMF), World Bank, and World Trade Organization (WTO) play crucial roles in promoting economic stability, providing financial support, and facilitating international trade and investment.

7. **Consumer Preferences and Cultural Exchange**: Increased global communication and travel have led to the convergence of consumer preferences and the spread of cultural products and services. This cultural exchange fosters demand for foreign goods and services, further driving globalization.
