Response 1: Economic globalization refers to the increasing interdependence of world economies as a result of the growing scale of cross-border trade of commodities and services, flow of international capital, and wide and rapid spread of technologies. Several key factors drive this process:

1. **Technological Advancements**: Innovations in transportation (like container shipping and air freight) and communication (such as the internet and mobile technology) have drastically reduced the costs and increased the speed of moving goods, services, information, and people across borders.

2. **Trade Liberalization**: The reduction of tariffs, quotas, and other trade barriers through international agreements (such as those under the World Trade Organization) has facilitated a freer flow of goods and services between countries.

3. **Investment and Capital Flows**: The deregulation of financial markets and the liberalization of capital controls have enabled a significant increase in cross-border investments, including foreign direct investment (FDI) and portfolio investment.

4. **Multinational Corporations (MNCs)**: These large companies operate in multiple countries, driving global production networks and supply chains. They often seek efficiencies by locating different stages of production in various countries based on cost, expertise, and resource availability.

5. **Economic Policies**: Many countries have adopted policies that encourage globalization, such as privatization, deregulation, and the creation of special economic zones (SEZs) to attract foreign investment.

6. **Global Supply Chains**: The fragmentation of production processes across different countries allows firms to exploit comparative advantages, leading to more efficient global supply chains.

7. **Labor Mobility**: While more restricted than the movement of goods and capital, the migration of labor, both skilled and unskilled, also contributes to economic globalization by addressing labor shortages and transferring skills and knowledge.

8. **Cultural Exchange and Global Media**: The spread of global media and cultural exchange fosters a global consumer culture, increasing demand for international goods and services.

9. **International Institutions**: Organizations such as the International Monetary Fund (IMF), World Bank, and regional trade agreements (like NAFTA, now USMCA, and the European Union) play significant roles in promoting and regulating economic globalization.

10. **Political Stability and Governance**: Countries with stable political environments and effective governance are more attractive to international investors and trade partners, facilitating greater economic integration.

These factors collectively contribute to the complex and dynamic process of economic globalization, shaping the global economic landscape and influencing national economies in profound ways.
