* **Qin Dynasty (221-206 BCE):** Emperor Qin Shi Huang unified China and ordered the connection of existing walls into a single, continuous fortification. This marked the beginning of the Great Wall as a national project.
* **Han Dynasty (206 BCE-220 CE):** The Han emperors extended the wall westward and eastward, reaching a length of over 10,000 kilometers.
* **Ming Dynasty (1368-1644):** The Ming emperors rebuilt and strengthened the wall, using bricks and stone instead of rammed earth. This period saw the construction of the most iconic sections of the wall, including the Badaling and Mutianyu sections.
* **Qing Dynasty (1644-1912):** The Qing emperors maintained the wall but did not make significant additions.

**Cultural Significance**

The Great Wall of China is a symbol of Chinese history, culture, and national pride. It represents:

* **Defense and Protection:** The wall was built primarily to protect China from nomadic tribes and other invaders. It served as a physical barrier and a deterrent to potential threats.
* **Engineering Marvel:** The wall is an architectural masterpiece, demonstrating the ingenuity and skill of Chinese engineers. Its construction required massive amounts of labor, materials, and resources.
* **National Unity:** The wall symbolizes the unity and strength of the Chinese people. It was built through the cooperation of different dynasties and ethnic groups.
* **Tourism and Heritage:** Today, the Great Wall is a major tourist destination and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It attracts millions of visitors annually who marvel at its grandeur and historical significance.
* **Cultural Symbol:** The wall has become an iconic symbol of China, featured in countless works of art, literature, and film. It represents the country's rich history, resilience, and cultural heritage.

**Legacy**

The Great Wall of China remains a testament to the ingenuity and determination of the Chinese people. It is a symbol of their national identity and a reminder of the challenges and triumphs they have faced throughout history. The wall continues to inspire awe and admiration, serving as a reminder of the human capacity for both defense and construction.
