Response 1: **Causes of the Great Depression**

* **Overproduction and excess supply:** The rapid industrialization of the 1920s led to overproduction in many industries, resulting in a surplus of goods and a drop in prices.
* **Stock market crash of 1929:** The collapse of the stock market on October 29, 1929, wiped out billions of dollars in wealth and eroded consumer confidence.
* **Weak banking system:** Many banks were poorly regulated and had made risky investments. When the stock market crashed, these banks failed, leading to a loss of public trust and a contraction of credit.
* **High tariffs:** The Smoot-Hawley Tariff of 1930 raised tariffs on imported goods, reducing international trade and exacerbating the global economic downturn.
* **Monetary policy:** The Federal Reserve raised interest rates to combat inflation, which further tightened credit and slowed economic growth.

**Effects of the Great Depression**

* **Mass unemployment:** By 1933, over 25% of the American workforce was unemployed, reaching a peak of 32.7% in 1934.
* **Economic decline:** National income fell by over 50% between 1929 and 1933, and industrial production declined by over 30%.
* **Social unrest:** The Depression led to widespread poverty, hunger, and homelessness. It also sparked social unrest, including protests and even riots.
* **Political instability:** The Depression weakened the legitimacy of governments worldwide and led to the rise of authoritarian regimes, such as Nazism in Germany.
* **Long-term economic consequences:** The Depression had lasting effects on the global economy, including a shift towards government intervention and social welfare programs.

**Additional Impacts:**

* **Deflation:** Falling prices during the Depression made it harder for businesses to pay off their debts and for consumers to afford goods.
* **Bank failures:** Over 9,000 banks failed during the Depression, eroding public confidence in the financial system.
* **Dust Bowl:** Severe droughts in the Great Plains during the 1930s exacerbated the economic crisis, causing widespread crop failures and forcing farmers to abandon their land.
* **Psychological toll:** The Depression had a profound impact on the mental health of individuals, leading to increased rates of depression, anxiety, and suicide.
