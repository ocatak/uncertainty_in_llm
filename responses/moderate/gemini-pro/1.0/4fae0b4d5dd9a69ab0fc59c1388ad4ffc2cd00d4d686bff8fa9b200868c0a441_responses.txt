**1. Climate Change:**

* Deforestation releases this carbon into the atmosphere, contributing to greenhouse gas emissions.
* This leads to global warming, altering weather patterns and causing extreme events.

**2. Loss of Biodiversity:**
* Forests are home to an estimated 80% of the world's terrestrial species.
* Deforestation destroys habitats, leading to the extinction of countless plants and animals.
* This loss of biodiversity disrupts ecosystems and affects food webs, ultimately impacting human health and well-being.

**3. Soil Erosion and Water Shortages:**
* Trees' roots help hold soil in place and prevent erosion.
* Deforestation leaves soil vulnerable to wind and rain, leading to degradation and loss.
* This erosion can pollute rivers and lead to water shortages, as forests act as watersheds and regulate water flow.

**4. Desertification:**
* Deforestation in dry areas can result in desertification, a process where fertile land becomes barren.
* Without trees to protect the soil from wind and sun, it loses moisture and becomes unproductive.
* This has severe consequences for food security and livelihoods in affected regions.

**5. Altered Precipitation Patterns:**
* Forests influence the local and regional climate by releasing water vapor into the atmosphere.
* Deforestation reduces this water vapor, affecting cloud formation and rainfall patterns.

* Trees absorb carbon dioxide and release oxygen during photosynthesis.
* Deforestation disrupts this cycle, reducing oxygen production and adding to the carbon dioxide surplus in the atmosphere.
