* There are two types of charges: positive and negative.
* Like charges repel each other, while opposite charges attract each other.

**2. Current:**
* Electric current is the flow of charged particles.
* It is measured in amperes (A).
* Current flows from a higher potential to a lower potential.

**3. Voltage:**
* Voltage is the difference in electrical potential between two points.
* It is measured in volts (V).
* Voltage causes charged particles to move.

**4. Resistance:**
* Resistance is the opposition to the flow of current.
* It is measured in ohms (Ω).
* Higher resistance reduces current flow.

**5. Ohm's Law:**
* Ohm's Law states that the current flowing through a conductor is directly proportional to the voltage and inversely proportional to the resistance.
* Mathematically, V = IR, where V is voltage, I is current, and R is resistance.

**6. Circuits:**
* An electrical circuit is a closed loop that allows current to flow.
* There are two types of circuits:
    * Series circuits: Components are connected in a single loop, with current flowing through each component in turn.
    * Parallel circuits: Components are connected in multiple loops, allowing current to flow through different paths.

**7. Power:**
* Electrical power is the rate at which electrical energy is transferred or consumed.
* It is measured in watts (W).
* Power is calculated as P = VI, where P is power, V is voltage, and I is current.

**8. Energy:**
* Electrical energy is the ability to do work or produce heat.
* It is measured in joules (J).
* Energy is calculated as E = Pt, where E is energy, P is power, and t is time.
