### 1. **Ingestion**
**Oral Cavity (Mouth):**
- **Teeth**: Chew food into smaller pieces, increasing the surface area for enzymes to work on.
- **Salivary Glands**: Release saliva containing the enzyme amylase, which begins starch digestion.
- **Tongue**: Helps in mixing food with saliva and forming it into a bolus (a small, round mass) for swallowing.

### 2. **Propulsion**
**Swallowing (Deglutition):**
- **Pharynx**: Transfers the bolus from the mouth to the esophagus.
- **Esophagus**: Moves the bolus to the stomach through peristaltic movements (wave-like muscle contractions).

### 3. **Mechanical and Chemical Digestion**
**Stomach:**
- **Mechanical Digestion**: The stomach muscles churn the food, mixing it with gastric juices to form a semi-liquid mixture called chyme.
- **Chemical Digestion**: Gastric juices contain hydrochloric acid (HCl) and the enzyme pepsin, which begin protein digestion and help kill bacteria.

### 4. **Absorption**
**Small Intestine:**
- **Duodenum**: The first part of the small intestine where most chemical digestion occurs with the help of bile (from the liver and stored in the gallbladder) and pancreatic juice (from the pancreas). Bile emulsifies fats, and pancreatic enzymes continue the breakdown of proteins, fats, and carbohydrates.
- **Jejunum and Ileum**: These sections are primarily involved in nutrient absorption. Villi and microvilli, tiny hair-like structures lining the intestines, increase the surface area to absorb nutrients into the bloodstream.

### 5. **Transport of Nutrients**
- **Bloodstream and Lymphatic System**: Absorbed nutrients are transported to the rest of the body. Carbohydrates and proteins enter the bloodstream, while fats enter the lymphatic system before eventually reaching the bloodstream.

### 6. **Absorption and Elimination**
**Large Intestine (Colon):**
- **Water and Electrolyte Absorption**: The colon absorbs water and electrolytes from the remaining indigestible food matter.
- **Bacterial Fermentation**: Harbors beneficial bacteria that further break down some undigested carbohydrates, producing gases and certain vitamins.
- **Formation of Feces**: The remaining indigestible material is formed into feces.

### 7. **Defecation**
**Rectum and Anus:**
- **Storage**: Feces are stored in the rectum until they are expelled through the anus.
- **Elimination**: The action of defecation expels waste material from the digestive tract.

Each stage is critical for ensuring that food is efficiently broken down, nutrients are absorbed, and waste products are eliminated from the body.
