Response 1: The life cycle of a butterfly is a fascinating process known as complete metamorphosis, which consists of four distinct stages: egg, larva (caterpillar), pupa (chrysalis), and adult butterfly. Here’s a detailed look at each stage:

1. **Egg**:
   - **Duration**: A few days to several weeks, depending on species and environmental conditions.
   - **Description**: Female butterflies lay eggs on host plants, which are specific plants that caterpillars of that species can eat. The eggs can be laid singly, in clusters, or in rows, and they are often tiny and can vary in shape and color depending on the species.

2. **Larva (Caterpillar)**:
   - **Duration**: A few weeks to a month.
   - **Description**: Once the egg hatches, a caterpillar emerges. This stage is characterized by rapid growth, as the caterpillar's primary job is to eat and store energy for the next stages of development. Caterpillars will molt several times (known as instars) as they grow, shedding their exoskeletons.

3. **Pupa (Chrysalis)**:
   - **Duration**: A few weeks to several months, depending on the species and environmental conditions.
   - **Description**: The caterpillar forms a pupa, or chrysalis, in which it undergoes a remarkable transformation. Inside the chrysalis, the caterpillar's body breaks down and reorganizes into the adult butterfly. This transformation is called metamorphosis.

4. **Adult Butterfly**:
   - **Duration**: A few weeks to several months, depending on the species.
   - **Description**: The adult butterfly emerges from the chrysalis with wings that are initially soft and crumpled. It will pump fluid into its wings to expand and harden them, which usually takes a few hours. Once the wings are fully dry and functional, the butterfly can fly. The adult butterfly’s primary roles are to mate and lay eggs, thereby continuing the life cycle.

**Additional Notes**:
- **Feeding**: As caterpillars, they are primarily herbivorous, feeding on leaves of their host plants. Adult butterflies typically feed on nectar from flowers but can also feed on other substances like fruit juices, tree sap, and even animal dung or carrion.
- **Behavior**: Butterflies often exhibit specific behaviors such as basking in the sun to warm up their flight muscles or migrating to find suitable climates and resources.

This complete metamorphosis allows butterflies to exploit different ecological niches during their life cycle, reducing competition between the larval and adult stages.

- **Duration**: The egg stage lasts from a few days to a couple of weeks, depending on the butterfly species and environmental conditions.
- **Appearance**: Butterfly eggs can vary in shape, size, and color but are generally tiny, often no larger than a pinhead.

### 2. Larva (Caterpillar)
- **Description**: When the egg hatches, a larva, commonly known as a caterpillar, emerges. The primary function of the caterpillar is to eat and grow.
- **Feeding**: Caterpillars are voracious eaters and will consume large amounts of leaves from their host plant.
- **Molting**: As the caterpillar grows, it will molt (shed its skin) several times. Each stage between molts is called an instar.
- **Duration**: The larval stage can last from a few weeks to a month or more, depending on the species and environmental conditions.

### 3. Pupa (Chrysalis)
- **Description**: Once the caterpillar has eaten enough and reached a certain size, it will enter the pupal stage. It forms a protective casing around itself called a chrysalis.
- **Metamorphosis**: Inside the chrysalis, the caterpillar undergoes a remarkable transformation. Its body breaks down into a sort of soup and reorganizes into the structures of an adult butterfly.
- **Duration**: The pupal stage can last from a week to several months, depending on the species and environmental factors like temperature.

### 4. Adult Butterfly
- **Emergence**: When the transformation is complete, the adult butterfly emerges from the chrysalis. Its wings are initially soft and crumpled but will expand and harden within a few hours.
- **Reproduction**: The adult butterfly's primary function is to reproduce. It will seek out a mate to continue the life cycle.
- **Feeding**: Adult butterflies feed on nectar from flowers, using a specialized mouthpart called a proboscis.
- **Lifespan**: The lifespan of an adult butterfly can range from a few weeks to several months, depending on the species.

### Summary
- **Egg**: Laid on host plants, lasts a few days to weeks.
- **Larva (Caterpillar)**: Eats and grows, lasts weeks to a month or more.
- **Pupa (Chrysalis)**: Undergoes metamorphosis, lasts a week to several months.
- **Adult Butterfly**: Emerges to reproduce and feed, lives for weeks to months.

This remarkable cycle ensures the continuation of butterfly species and contributes to the biodiversity and ecological health of their environments.
