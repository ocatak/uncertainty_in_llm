   - **Description**: The life cycle begins when a female butterfly lays eggs, usually on the leaves of host plants.

2. **Larva (Caterpillar)**:
   - **Description**: Once the egg hatches, a caterpillar emerges. This is the feeding and growth stage.
   - **Duration**: This stage can last from a few weeks to a month or more, depending on the species.
   - **Characteristics**: Caterpillars are voracious eaters, consuming large amounts of leaves to fuel their rapid growth. They molt several times, shedding their exoskeleton to accommodate their increasing size.

3. **Pupa (Chrysalis)**:
   - **Description**: After reaching a certain size, the caterpillar forms a pupa, also known as a chrysalis. This is the transformation stage.
   - **Duration**: This stage can last from a few weeks to several months, depending on the species and environmental conditions.
   - **Characteristics**: Inside the chrysalis, the caterpillar undergoes a remarkable transformation called metamorphosis. The tissues and organs of the caterpillar are broken down and reformed into the structures of an adult butterfly.

4. **Adult Butterfly**:
   - **Description**: The final stage is the emergence of the adult butterfly from the chrysalis.
   - **Duration**: Adult butterflies can live from a few weeks to several months, depending on the species.
   - **Characteristics**: Adult butterflies have fully developed wings and are capable of flight. They focus on feeding (primarily on nectar) and reproduction. After mating, the female lays eggs, and the cycle begins anew.
