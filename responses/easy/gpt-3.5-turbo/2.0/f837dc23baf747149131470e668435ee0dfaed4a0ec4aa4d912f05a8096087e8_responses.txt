Response 11: One explanation for how airplanes stay in the air is known as Bernoulli's Principle. This principle states that an increase in the speed of a fluid (such as air) results in a decrease in pressure. An airplane's design includes wings that are shaped in such a way that as the airplane moves forward through the air, the air passing over the top of the wing has to travel a longer distance than the air passing under the wing. This creates a pressure difference, with lower pressure on the top of the wing and higher pressure underneath. This pressure difference creates lift, allowing an airplane to stay in the air and maneuver through the atmosphere.
