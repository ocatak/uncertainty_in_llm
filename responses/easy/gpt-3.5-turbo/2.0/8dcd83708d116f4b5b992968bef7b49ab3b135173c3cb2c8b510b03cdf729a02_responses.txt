Response 42: A microwave oven cooks food by producing electromagnetic waves in the microwave frequency range. These microwaves penetrate the food and cause the molecules within to vibrate and generate heat. This heat then cooks the food from the inside out, leading to quick and efficient cooking.

The microwaves are directed by a specialized piece inside the microwave cavity which distributes the waves evenly within the space. The turntable further helps to ensure even cooking by rotating the dish so that all parts of the food are exposed to the microwaves.

Microwaves are particularly effective at heating up food rich in water and fats, as these molecules absorb the microwaves more readily and cook faster. This cooking method allows for gentle and fast cooking without over-drying the food.
