Response 45: Cellular respiration is the metabolic process by which cells break down sugars such as glucose to release energy in the form of adenosine triphosphate (ATP). The process of cellular respiration consists of three main stages: glycolysis, the citric acid cycle (also known as the Krebs cycle), and oxidative phosphorylation.

1. Glycolysis: The first stage of respiration takes place in the cytoplasm and does not require oxygen. In this stage, glucose is broken down into pyruvate in a series of enzyme-catalyzed reactions. ATP and NADH are also generated during glycolysis.

2. Citric acid cycle: Pyruvate molecules formed from glycolysis enter the mitochondria, where they undergo a series of oxidation reactions to generate energy-carrying molecules like NADH and FADH2. These high-energy molecules are then used in the next stage of respiration to produce ATP.
