The heart consists of four chambers - the two upper chambers, known as the atria, and the two lower chambers, known as the ventricles. The atria receive blood from the body and lungs, while the ventricles pump the blood out to the body and lungs. This continuous pumping action ensures that all the cells in the body receive the necessary nutrients and oxygen for survival. The heart also plays a role in regulating blood pressure and maintaining the body's overall circulation. In summary, the function of the heart is to circulate blood throughout the body, ensuring that all organs and tissues receive the necessary nutrients and oxygen to function properly.
