The byproduct of the light-dependent reactions is oxygen, which is released as a waste product into the surrounding environment.

In the Calvin cycle, CO2 is fixed and converted into glucose, utilizing the energy carried by the previously formed molecules. The glucose produced serves as a source of energy for the algal cells to carry out cellular processes essential for survival. 

In summary, photosynthesis in algae involves the absorption of light energy, the production of ATP and NADPH, the use of these molecules in the Calvin cycle to fix CO2 and produce glucose, and the release of oxygen as a byproduct.
