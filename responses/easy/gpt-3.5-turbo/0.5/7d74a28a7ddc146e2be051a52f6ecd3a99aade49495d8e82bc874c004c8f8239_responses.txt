Response 1: A washing machine cleans clothes by combining several key elements: water, detergent, agitation, and spinning. 

1. Water: The first step in the cleaning process is to fill the washing machine with water. The water helps to loosen dirt, stains, and grime from the fabric of the clothes.

2. Detergent: Once the water is added, detergent is also added to help break down and remove dirt and stains from the clothes. The detergent works by bonding with oils and dirt particles, allowing them to be rinsed away during the washing process.

3. Agitation: The next step is agitation, which is the back-and-forth motion of the agitator or drum inside the washing machine. This motion helps to dislodge dirt and stains from the fabric of the clothes, allowing the detergent and water to work more effectively.

4. Spinning: After the clothes have been agitated, the washing machine will spin at a high speed to remove excess water from the clothes. This helps to speed up the drying process and ensures that the clothes are not too wet when they are removed from the machine.

Overall, the combination of water, detergent, agitation, and spinning work together to effectively clean clothes in a washing machine.
