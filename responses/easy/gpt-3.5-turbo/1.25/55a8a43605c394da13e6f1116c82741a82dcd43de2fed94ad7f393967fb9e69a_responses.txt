Response 1: Evaporation and condensation are two phases of the water cycle that involve the transformation of water from a liquid to a gas (evaporation) and from a gas to a liquid (condensation).

Evaporation occurs when water molecules in a liquid state gain enough energy to escape into the air as water vapor. This typically happens when a body of water is heated by the sun, or when a liquid is exposed to dry air. When this occurs, the water molecules move faster and overcome the forces holding them together, eventually escaping as water vapor. This process is key to the water cycle as it allows for water to be transported from liquid bodies to the atmosphere.

Condensation, on the other hand, occurs when water vapor in the air cools down and loses energy, causing the water molecules to come together and form liquid droplets. This typically happens when warm, moist air comes into contact with a cooler surface or environment, such as when warm air meets a cold window pane and forms condensation. Condensation is also responsible for the formation of clouds, which are made up of water droplets that have condensed around tiny particles in the atmosphere.

Overall, the processes of evaporation and condensation are closely linked and are crucial for the water cycle as they help to regulate the distribution of water on Earth.
