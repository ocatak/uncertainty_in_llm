Response 1: Plants absorb nutrients from the soil through their root system. The roots of a plant have tiny root hairs that increase the surface area available for nutrient absorption. These root hairs come into contact with the soil particles, where essential nutrients are found in the form of ions.

The process of nutrient absorption occurs through several mechanisms. Firstly, nutrients are taken up by the root hairs through passive diffusion, where they move from an area of high concentration in the soil to an area of low concentration in the root cells. This process is facilitated by the concentration gradient between the soil and the root cells.

Secondly, plants also use active transport to absorb nutrients from the soil. This process requires energy and specific transporter proteins in the root cell membranes. Active transport allows plants to absorb nutrients against their concentration gradient, ensuring that they can obtain essential nutrients even when they are present in low concentrations in the soil.

Overall, the root system of a plant plays a crucial role in nutrient absorption from the soil, allowing the plant to obtain the essential nutrients it needs for growth, development, and reproduction.
